# ANNOUNCEMENT: Invitation for Institutional Participation in Sovereign Stewardship Consultation

## Background and Context
Following recent, robust discussions at the Center for Information Technology Policy at Princeton University, it has become increasingly clear that the path toward secure, reliable AI requires a paradigm shift. The discourse highlighted a critical need for AI frameworks that prioritize "Governance-First" architectures, moving beyond simple token scaling toward verifiable, human-accountable stewardship.

To build on these insights, we are expanding our ongoing Public Consultation to include additional institutions, government agencies, and corporate entities who recognize that the future of AI infrastructure must be grounded in ethical integrity and rigorous human oversight.

---

## Why Institutional Partnership Matters
Our current consultation has identified three non-negotiable pillars for next-generation AI:

1. **Meaningful Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Oversight:** Moving beyond symbolic compliance to ensure that human operators maintain final authority and responsibility for high-stakes decisions.
2. 
3. **"Do No Harm" Stewardship:** Embedding ethical guardrails directly into recursive agent operations, ensuring that technological progress remains aligned with the safety of humans, businesses, and future generations.
4. 
5. **Institutional Asset Compounding:** Transitioning AI from a transient cost center into a long-term, verifiable asset that grows in precision and value through human-verified ground truth.
